Projects

Split.it

HCI Project

This project was developed during Fall 2018 for the Human-Computer Interaction course. For our project, me and another 2 group members created a high-fidelity prototype of a web-based system using web technologies such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript and jQuery. Our website helps a group of friends, family or co-workers to split a bill with ease. The user will simply go on the website and fill in their information as a group and then decide which method they would like to split the bill.

This could be used for anything related to paying in groups like going to a restaurant, grocery store, splitting rent, a trip expense or owing a friend money. If the group decided one single person will pay for everybody, the system will randomly select one who is able to pay. Then, it will keep track of who payed a bill and how much was it. If a person owns an amount to someone that payed for them, the value will be saved. If the group decided to split it evenly, they won’t need to calculate anything, the website will do it for them.

The Computer Science Experience

Software Engeneering Project

Spanning only 30 in-game days, The C.S. Experience is a short, simulation-RPG hybrid videogame in which players assume the role of a computer science student and are fast-tracked through a single university term that demands them to manage their time, character health, studies and other factors in order to pass their exams.

The weeks leading up to each exam period will present the player with a large degree of freedom and should be used to prepare for exams by engaging in character-developing activities. When it is time to write an exam, the player enters a turn-based style combat scenario, similar to Pokemon or Final Fantasy. A well-prepared player, who’s improved his or her skills by studying the course material, and has maintained his or her character’s happiness, sleep, and hunger values will have an easier ‘combat encounter’ with the exam, most likely receiving a higher grade.
